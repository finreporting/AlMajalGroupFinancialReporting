📋 AP AGING — EXECUTIVE COMMENTARY (April 2026)
As of 30 April 2026, NGK's total accounts payable stands at $1,503,922 — entirely concentrated with a single supplier, NGK Spark Plugs Co. Ltd. (Japan), the parent brand-licensor. The full balance sits in the "121+ days" bucket ($1,503,922). AP has decreased $300K from March ($1,803,922) indicating a payment was made to reduce the balance. All AP is now classified as overdue based on aging date methodology.
⚠ Comment 1 — Single-Vendor Concentration (100% Exposure)
100% of AP ($1.80M) is owed to NGK Spark Plugs Japan. While this is expected for a brand-authorized distributor, it creates supply-chain and currency concentration risk:
  • Supply risk: any disruption at NGK Japan (strikes, port delays, Japan trade policy) would halt replenishment — current on-hand inventory ($3.34M) provides ~10.5 months of cover, mitigating near-term risk.
  • FX risk: invoicing is USD-denominated, which matches USD revenue collection — minimal translation exposure.
  • Negotiation leverage: the subsidiary has low bargaining power given sole-source dependency; price/terms changes from parent would flow through directly to margin.
✓ Comment 2 — Extraordinary DPO of 172 Days (Free Supplier Financing)
DPO of 132 days remains a significant working-capital lever. Against YTD COGS of ~$1.36M (~$340K/month), the $1.50M AP balance is equivalent to ~4.4 months of cost of goods financed by the supplier. This is atypical of third-party distribution and reflects the parent-subsidiary relationship — NGK Japan effectively extends open-ended credit to its Iraq arm.

Capital structure implication: this $1.50M supplier float funds roughly half of the $2.93M inventory position. Without it, NGK would need $1.50M of external debt — instead, the subsidiary runs with zero bank borrowings and a CCC of ~156 days supported by supplier DPO of 132d. The $300K payment in April demonstrates responsible vendor management.
💰 Comment 3 — Future Remit Composition
The $1,503,922 121+ balance represents invoices that have aged beyond standard terms. Despite the overdue classification, this is a parent-subsidiary arrangement with implicit extended credit. The $300K payment in April shows active payable management. Liquidity to service remaining payables will come from inventory turnover, AR collections ($555K gross), and intercompany settlement ($1.52M). The company continues to hold minimal cash, deploying it into inventory ($2.93M).
⚠ Comment 4 — Credit Balance ($25,838) Requires Action
April AP balance of $1,503,922 is down $300K from March ($1,803,922), indicating a payment was made. The credit balance has been resolved. Recommended actions: (1) negotiate a structured repayment schedule with NGK Japan, (2) align future payments with inventory turnover to manage cash flow, (3) maintain clean reconciliation for Q2 2026 close.
🎯 Comment 5 — Strategic & Risk Management Recommendations
  1. Preserve supplier trust: NGK's 172-day DPO is a privileged position — ensure no payments slip overdue even if cash gets tight; the value of supplier float (~$90K/yr equivalent interest savings at 6%) depends on relationship preservation.
  2. Formalize payment calendar: publish a 6-month rolling remittance schedule to NGK Japan so both sides can forecast cash; avoid ad-hoc payment patterns.
  3. Hedge volumetric concentration: explore adding a secondary line (already started — Yulim carries $57K / 1.7% of stock) to reduce single-supplier dependency over 24 months.
  4. Inventory pacing: with 8.6 months of stock on hand and 132-day payment terms, the next replenishment cycle is internally funded — pause ordering until stock drops to 4–5 months to reduce carrying cost.
  5. Intercompany netting: consider formally netting the $1.50M AP against the $1.52M Due-from-Allied ($16K net intercompany receivable) in consolidation to simplify reporting and improve optically-visible working capital ratios.
  6. Audit reconciliation: maintain clean reconciliation and document the $300K payment made in April for audit trail.
📊 Comment 6 — Period-over-Period Trend (AP vs Inventory)
April 2026 AP of $1.50M compares to March 2026 AP of $1.80M (-16.6%), reflecting the $300K payment. The AP-to-Inventory coverage ratio is 51% — meaning supplier credit funds just over half the stock position. This ratio is within the 45–55% historical range. The remaining $1.43M of inventory funding comes from retained equity + allied-receivable float. DPO of 132 days (vs 172 in March) reflects active vendor management.
