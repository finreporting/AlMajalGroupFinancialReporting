📋 AP AGING — EXECUTIVE COMMENTARY (March 2026)
As of 31 March 2026, NGK's total accounts payable stands at $1,803,922 — entirely concentrated with a single supplier, NGK Spark Plugs Co. Ltd. (Japan), the parent brand-licensor. The full balance sits in the "Future Remit" bucket ($1,829,759) offset by a small 0–30 day credit of ($25,838). There are zero overdue payables in any bucket — a clean, disciplined payables book.
⚠ Comment 1 — Single-Vendor Concentration (100% Exposure)
100% of AP ($1.80M) is owed to NGK Spark Plugs Japan. While this is expected for a brand-authorized distributor, it creates supply-chain and currency concentration risk:
  • Supply risk: any disruption at NGK Japan (strikes, port delays, Japan trade policy) would halt replenishment — current on-hand inventory ($3.34M) provides ~10.5 months of cover, mitigating near-term risk.
  • FX risk: invoicing is USD-denominated, which matches USD revenue collection — minimal translation exposure.
  • Negotiation leverage: the subsidiary has low bargaining power given sole-source dependency; price/terms changes from parent would flow through directly to margin.
✓ Comment 2 — Extraordinary DPO of 172 Days (Free Supplier Financing)
DPO of 172 days is the single most valuable working-capital lever on the balance sheet. Against YTD COGS of ~$953K (~$317K/month), the $1.80M AP balance is equivalent to ~5.7 months of cost of goods financed by the supplier at zero interest. This is atypical of third-party distribution and reflects the parent-subsidiary relationship — NGK Japan effectively extends open-ended credit to its Iraq arm.

Capital structure implication: this $1.80M supplier float funds roughly half of the $3.34M inventory position. Without it, NGK would need $1.80M of external debt or equity injection — instead, the subsidiary runs with zero bank borrowings and a manageable CCC of ~192 days supported by supplier DPO of 170d.
💰 Comment 3 — Future Remit Composition
The $1,829,759 Future Remit represents invoices booked but contractually not yet due — standard 90/120/180-day net terms extended by the parent. This is not overdue and not a credit event; it is scheduled supplier financing. The company currently holds minimal cash at quarter-end, having deployed operating cash into inventory ($3.34M) and intercompany receivable ($1.52M). Liquidity to service these payables will come from inventory turnover, AR collections ($626K gross), and intercompany settlement — all of which cycle on a quarterly cadence.
⚠ Comment 4 — Credit Balance ($25,838) Requires Action
The ($25,838) credit balance in 0–30 days indicates either an overpayment, returned goods, or unmatched credit memo from NGK Spark Plugs. Recommended immediate actions: (1) issue a formal reconciliation request to NGK Japan AR team, (2) obtain credit-memo documentation for audit trail, (3) apply against the next invoice release to clean the ledger before Q2 2026 close.
🎯 Comment 5 — Strategic & Risk Management Recommendations
  1. Preserve supplier trust: NGK's 172-day DPO is a privileged position — ensure no payments slip overdue even if cash gets tight; the value of free float (~$108K/yr equivalent interest savings at 6%) depends on relationship preservation.
  2. Formalize payment calendar: publish a 6-month rolling remittance schedule to NGK Japan so both sides can forecast cash; avoid ad-hoc payment patterns.
  3. Hedge volumetric concentration: explore adding a secondary line (already started — Yulim carries $57K / 1.7% of stock) to reduce single-supplier dependency over 24 months.
  4. Inventory pacing: with 10.5 months of stock on hand and 172-day payment terms, the entire next replenishment cycle is internally funded — pause ordering until stock drops to 4–5 months to avoid excess carrying cost.
  5. Intercompany netting: consider formally netting the $1.80M AP against the $1.52M Due-from-Allied ($283K net intercompany payable) in consolidation to simplify reporting and improve optically-visible working capital ratios.
  6. Audit reconciliation: resolve the ($25,838) credit balance before March 2026 audit close to avoid qualified opinion risk on AP completeness.
📊 Comment 6 — Period-over-Period Trend (AP vs Inventory)
March 2026 AP of $1.80M compares to Dec 2025 AP of ~$1.67M (+7.8%), reflecting the Q1 inventory build-up ($3.34M vs ~$2.8M FY25 close).
